Why this Matter?

Insufficient manpower for software engineers and data foundation knowledge has resulted in technical debt at the core of software development - the data. The next big challenge for companies is organizational and centered around data. A "Modern Data Act 2" is needed to engineer data foundations and improve data quality standards in the era of increasing AI. Companies that don't keep up with these changes will fall behind.

Data quality is the foundation upon which data science rests. Without reliable and accurate data, any insights or decisions derived from data analysis are likely to be flawed. In fact, poor data quality can lead to significant business losses and damage to reputation. Therefore, it is imperative for companies to prioritize data quality when undertaking any data-related initiatives.
Currently, the field of data science is experiencing a gap when it comes to data quality. Despite its importance, many organizations still do not have a formalized approach to data quality. Some may simply rely on the data that is readily available to them without considering the reliability of the sources, while others may have a quality control process in place that is not rigorous enough to detect all errors and inconsistencies in the data.
Additionally, while there are numerous case studies and examples available online that showcase the application of data science in a business context, very few of them provide a comprehensive view of the data science process. Often, these examples highlight the technical aspects of data science, without adequately addressing the crucial business and operational considerations that underpin any successful data science project.
